Overview

After taking some lectures and checking several materials, it took me a long time to understand the basics of exponential family and why and how they can be applied to GLM(Generalized Linear Models), which is also the goal of this article.

We will begin with exponential families, then cover the basics of some machine learning algorithms(like linear regression and logistic regression) and observe nice properties. In the end, we will integrate all these things together, to derive a larger scale of models and to understand the reasons behind the nice properties."

Exponential Family

Definition

The exponential familiy involve a set of probabilistic distributions whose probability density function can be expressed as \[\begin{equation} \label{eq1} p(y | \eta) = h(y) \exp(\eta^T T(y) - a(\eta)) \end{equation}\]

Another definition is given by \[\begin{equation} \label{eq2} p(y | \theta) = h(y) \exp(\mathrm{H} (\theta)^T T(y) - A(\theta)) \end{equation}\]

Actually, \(\eqref{eq1}\) is the canonical form of \(\eqref{eq2}\), with \(\eta = \mathrm{H} (\theta)\) and \[a(\eta) = \log \int h(x) \exp(\eta^T T(y))\]

Properties

Property 1: \(\frac {\partial a(\eta)} {\partial \eta} = \mathrm{E}(T(y))\)

Property 2: \(\frac {\partial^2 a(\eta)} {\partial \eta^2} = \mathrm{Var}(T(y))\)

Property 3: Let \(\mathcal{L}(\eta | y) = \log p(y | \eta)\) be the log-likelihood function, then \[\begin{aligned} \frac {\partial \mathcal{L}(\eta | y)} {\partial \eta} &= T(y) - \frac {\partial a(\eta)} {\partial \eta} \\ &= T(y) - E(T(y)) \end{aligned} \]

Applications

Using the properties mentioned above, we can compute the mean and the variance of a probability distribution in the exponential family in a simpler way.

Consider the normal distribution with \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\). For convenience, we assume \(\sigma\) is a constant and the normal distribution is only parameterized by \(\mu\).

\[\begin{aligned} p(x; \mu) &= \frac {1} {\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left(-\frac {(x - \mu)^2} {2\sigma^2} \right) \\ &= \frac {\exp(-\frac {x^2} {\sigma^2})} {\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} \exp \left(\frac {x\mu} {\sigma^2} - \frac {\mu^2} {2\sigma^2} \right) \end{aligned}\]

Let \(T(x) = \frac {x} {\sigma^2}, \ a(\mu) = \frac {\mu^2} {2\sigma^2}\), then

\[ \frac {\partial a(\mu)} {\partial \mu} = \frac {\mu} {\sigma^2} = \mathrm{E}\left(\frac {x} {\sigma^2} \right) \\ \frac {\partial^2 a(\mu)} {\partial \mu^2} = \frac {1} {\sigma^2} = \mathrm{Var}\left(\frac {x} {\sigma^2}\right)\]

One can see that the mean and the variance of the normal distribution are \(\mu\) and \(\sigma^2\) respectively.
